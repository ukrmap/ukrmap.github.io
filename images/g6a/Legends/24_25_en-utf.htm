Polar desert. Because of the severe climate vegetation is practically absent. Only in summer on islands and along the coasts of Antarctica are `patches of moss and lichen.

Tundra and lisotundry. Because of low temperatures and excessive zvolozhenist vast expanse covered with swamps, formed on top of permafrost. In mosses and lichens occur here dwarf birch and arctic willow, many summer berries. Further south are the `zone of birch, fir, larch.

Forests. The climate is warmer than the tundra. If significant rainfall distributed fairly rich woody vegetation. In a coniferous taiga spruce, fir, pine, larch, further south - leaved birch, aspen, oak, maple, linden, elm. Also common herb and shrub `herbaceous vegetation.

Steppes and forest steppes. Summer is hot and dry, winters usually malosnezhnye, sometimes severe frosts occur. Predominates herbs herbaceous vegetation ', because of the trees is not enough moisture. Small woodlands are concentrated along river valleys and near the water and presented mainly deciduous tree species.

Deserts and semi-desert. The climate is dry and hot with possible large fluctuations in temperature throughout the day. Due to the lack of moisture often not continuous vegetation cover. Few flora represented by camel-thorn, haloxylon, cacti and other plants to moisture unpretentious.

Savannah and woodlands. High temperatures throughout the year. Most precipitation falls in summer, when high (up to 5 m) continuous blanket of grass covering the surface of the shroud. Trees certain groups (in Africa - acacia, baobab). In the dry winter season resets most of the trees leaves and dry grass.

Zhorstkolysti Mediterranean forests and shrub-sea type. Under warm and wet winter and hot summer developed a small plant with hard leaves, which reduces evaporation. Most typical of the Mediterranean forests are evergreen oaks, bay and arbutus, pine trees, shrubs, and sequoia (Pacific coast of North America).

Wet forests. Formed in a hot and humid climate. Very rich flora: rubber tree, wife of the artist, palms, lianas and others. Wet equatorial forests emit too much oxygen in the atmosphere, so they called the planet's lungs.
